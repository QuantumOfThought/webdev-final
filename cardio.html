Cardio Training Programs

Cardio is one of the best ways to improve heart health, reduce stress, and boost endurance. For college students, incorporating cardio into your weekly routine can help you manage stress, improve focus, and even sleep better. With hectic schedules and academic pressures, cardio provides a physical outlet to maintain both mental and physical balance.

Couch to 5K Running Program
Week Workouts Notes
1 20 min walk/run intervals (30s run, 90s walk) Focus on building consistency.
2 20 min walk/run intervals (60s run, 90s walk) Gradually increase running time.
3 25 min walk/run intervals (90s run, 2 min walk) Keep the pace comfortable.
4 25 min walk/run intervals (3 min run, 2 min walk) Building stamina step by step.
5 25 min (5 min run, 3 min walk) Halfway to continuous running!
6 30 min (8 min run, 3 min walk) Focus on recovery during walks.
7 30 min (12 min run, 2 min walk) You're getting close to 5K readiness.
8 Continuous 30 min running Celebrate your accomplishment!
1K to 10K Rowing Training Program
Week Workouts Notes
1 3x500m intervals, 2 min rest Focus on technique and steady pacing.
2 4x750m intervals, 2 min rest Increase endurance with controlled effort.
3 5x1K intervals, 90s rest Focus on maintaining consistent splits.
4 4x2K intervals, 3 min rest Building longer endurance sets.
5 6K steady row, moderate pace Focus on steady-state endurance.
6 7K steady row, controlled pace Build confidence for longer rows.
7 8K row with negative splits Gradually increase intensity at the end.
8 10K continuous row Celebrate achieving 10K!

For more detailed rowing training plans, visit Concept2: The Olympic standard in rowers and rower training.
